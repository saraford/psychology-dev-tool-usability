The Psychology of Developer Tool Usability

In 2015, I received my Masters degree in Human Factors (read: User Experience in HCI) because I wanted to learn how to make developer tools usable. I've been building developer tools for over 15 years. After graduating with degrees in Computer Science and Mathematics, I joined Microsoft's Visual Studio team. I first got interested in developer tool usability after leading the accessibility and UX consistency testing efforts for Visual Studio 2005. But it was when I was the PM for CodePlex, Microsoft's open source project hosting site, where I learned the power of user-centered design and proper research methods from working with a UX consulting firm. I decided I had to learn how this UX consulting firm did their job, so I went back to grad school to learn all about psychology, research methods, kinesiology and motor learning, and of course proper office desk ergonomics. :)

This website contains

  • content from my latest talks on the psychology of developer tool usability
  • takeaways from research papers studying the usability of developer tools, especially IDEs
  • useful notes from grad school
